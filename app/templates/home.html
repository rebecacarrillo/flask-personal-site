About Me: fast facts

  • I like being alive

I'm interested in the study of complex networks, especially living ones.

During my undergrad, I became interested in the way organized crime networks can be shown, like many other human systems, to follow natural laws. For my bachelor's thesis, I designed and implemented a multidimensional network analysis model for understanding patterns in the inter-network activity of Mexican drug trafficking cartels. Mathematically, I found that information and activity travels through these criminal networks in much the same way that information travels in unicellular, nonhierarchical organisms, specifically following similar behavior patterns as the metabolic pathway in some bacterial cells. I'm interested in learning more about whether this quality is unique to illict organizations, or observable in other common social structures.

The framework for this project was done in R, Python and GEPHI

When I'm not writing code, I'm usually on the mats.
